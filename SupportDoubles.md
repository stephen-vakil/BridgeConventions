# Support Doubles and Redoubles

Used by opener to show 3-card support for responder's major when opponents interfere.

---

## Support Double

**When it applies:** Opener has opened, responder bid a major, and an opponent overcalls (through 2♠).

**Auction example:** 1♣ – (P) – 1<span style="color:red;">♥</span> – (1♠) – **Dbl** = opener has exactly 3 hearts

| Opener's action | Meaning |
|----------------|---------|
| Double | Exactly 3-card support for responder's major |
| Raise to 2M | 4-card support |

**Why it matters:**
Responder now knows whether to expect 3- or 4-card support when bidding game or slam, which affects how they evaluate their hand.

## Support Redouble

**When it applies:** Same situation as the support double, but the opponent **doubles** instead of overcalling.

**Auction example:** 1♣ – (P) – 1<span style="color:red;">♥</span> – (Dbl) – **Rdbl** = opener has 3-card support for hearts

| Opener's action | Meaning |
|----------------|---------|
| Redouble | 3-card support for responder's major |
| Raise to 2M | 4-card support |

**Applies through:** 2♠ (same as support double)

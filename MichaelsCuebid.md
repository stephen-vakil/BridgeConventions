# Michaels Cuebid

A cuebid of opener's suit to show a two-suited hand.

---

## The Convention

After an opponent opens 1 of a suit, a **direct cuebid** (2 of opener's suit) shows a specific two-suiter:

| Opponent opens | Your cuebid | Shows |
|---------------|-------------|-------|
| 1♣ | 2♣ | Both majors (5+ hearts and 5+ spades) |
| 1<span class="r">♦</span> | 2<span class="r">♦</span> | Both majors (5+ hearts and 5+ spades) |
| 1<span class="r">♥</span> | 2<span class="r">♥</span> | Spades + an unspecified minor |
| 1♠ | 2♠ | Hearts + an unspecified minor |

---

## Point Range / Strength

Generally a strong enough hand that we might win the auction.  If you expect opponents will win the auction
because your hand is so weak, don't bid Michael's because it gives the opponents too much information.

---

## Responses by Partner (Advancer)

| Advancer bid | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 2M (known major) | Preference, not forcing |
| 3M (known major) | Invitational |
| 4M (known major) | To play |
| 2NT | Asks for the minor (if applicable) |

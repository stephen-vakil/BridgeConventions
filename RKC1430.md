# Roman Keycard Blackwood (RKC 1430)

Used to count key cards (the four aces plus the king of trumps) before bidding slam.

---

## The Ask

**4NT** = asking for key cards (must have a clearly established trump suit)

The "1430" naming refers to the responses:

| Response | Key cards held |
|----------|---------------|
| 5♣ | 1 or 4 key cards |
| 5<span class="r">♦</span> | 0 or 3 key cards |
| 5<span class="r">♥</span> | 2 key cards, **no** trump queen |
| 5♠ | 2 key cards, **with** trump queen |
| 5NT | 2 key cards and a **void** |

---

## The Queen Ask

After a 5♣ or 5<span class="r">♦</span> response:
- **5 of trumps** = sign off (not interested in the queen)
- **Cheapest bid that is not trumps** = queen ask

| Response to queen ask | Meaning |
|-----------------------|---------|
| 5 of trumps (or 6 if forced) | No trump queen |
| 6 of trumps | Has trump queen |

**Examples:**
- Trumps are **spades**, response 5♣: 5<span class="r">♦</span> (queen ask) – 5♠ (no) / 6♠ (yes). Sign off = 5♠ directly.
- Trumps are **spades**, response 5<span class="r">♦</span>: 5<span class="r">♥</span> (queen ask) – 5♠ (no) / 6♠ (yes). Sign off = 5♠ directly.
- Trumps are **hearts**, response 5♣: 5<span class="r">♦</span> (queen ask) – 5<span class="r">♥</span> (no) / 6<span class="r">♥</span> (yes). Sign off = 5<span class="r">♥</span> directly.
- Trumps are **hearts**, response 5<span class="r">♦</span>: 5♠ (queen ask) – but now "no" = 6<span class="r">♥</span>, so you're already at slam.

*The queen ask can force you to the 6-level — this happens with hearts (after a 5<span class="r">♦</span> response) and always with minors. Only ask for the queen when you can afford to be at 6 if the answer is no, or when trumps are spades (always safe). This is part of why the guidelines below warn against using RKC when you could be forced past 5 of trumps.*

---

## When to Use / When NOT to Use RKC

**Use 4NT when:**
- Trump suit is clearly agreed or unambiguously implied
- You have no two fast losers in any side suit
- You're in slam-investigation range and need keycard information

**Don't use 4NT when:**
- **You have a void** — partner's key card in your void suit is worthless, and you can't evaluate accurately. Use cue bids instead, or Exclusion Blackwood if you play it.
- **No trump suit is agreed** — 4NT is ambiguous and might be natural/quantitative after a NT opening or when no suit has been bid and supported.
- **You're not in slam range** — don't fish for information you won't act on.
- **You'd be forced past 5 of trumps** — if the response could land you at the 6-level with two keycards missing, don't ask.
- **After 1NT or 2NT** without an agreed fit — 4NT is quantitative, not Blackwood.
- **Partner has shown a very weak hand** — Blackwood can't fix a bad trump suit or missing controls in side suits.

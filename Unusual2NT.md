# Unusual 2NT

A jump to 2NT over an opponent's opening bid to show the **two lowest unbid suits**.

---

## The Convention

**2NT** (jump) over an opponent's opening = 5+ cards in each of the two lowest unbid suits

| Opponent opens | Your 2NT shows |
|---------------|---------------|
| 1<span class="r">♥</span> | Clubs and diamonds (the two lowest unbid suits) |
| 1♠ | Clubs and diamonds |
| 1♣ | Diamonds and hearts |
| 1<span class="r">♦</span> | Clubs and hearts |

---

## Point Range / Strength

Unusual 2NT is primarily a **shape bid**, not an HCP bid — suit quality and distribution matter more than point count.

| Vulnerability | Typical strength |
|--------------|-----------------|
| Not vulnerable | ~6–11 HCP; prioritize suit quality over points |
| Vulnerable | ~8–11+ HCP; suits should be headed by at least a Q or better |

With 12+ HCP and 5-5 shape, the hand may be strong enough to act differently (e.g., overcall then bid again). Very strong 5-5 hands should usually not preempt with 2NT — they deserve more.

---

## Responses by Partner (Advancer)

Advancer **must** choose a suit — the 2NT bidder has shown 5-5, so partner picks the better fit.

| Advancer bid | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 3 of lower suit | Preference, to play; weak hand (0–8) |
| 3 of higher suit | Preference, to play; weak hand (0–8) |
| 4 of lower suit | Preemptive; good fit, weak hand |
| 4 of higher suit | Preemptive; good fit, weak hand |
| Cue bid of opponent's suit | Game-forcing; asks 2NT bidder to clarify better/longer suit |

**Cue bid = "I want to be in game, tell me more."** The 2NT bidder rebids the longer or better suit, which advancer converts to game.

---

## Unusual 2NT vs. Natural 2NT

A 2NT overcall is **unusual** (showing two-suited hand) when made as a direct jump over an opening bid at the 1-level.

A 2NT bid is **natural** (strong, balanced) when:
- Made after an opponent's weak two-bid (e.g., 2♠ – **2NT** = natural, strong balanced 15–18)
- Made as a response or rebid in an uncontested auction

When in doubt: if a natural 2NT makes sense given the auction, treat it as natural. If it's a jump overcall at the 2-level over a 1-level opening, it's unusual.

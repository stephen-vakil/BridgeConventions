# New Minor Forcing

Used by responder after a 1NT rebid by opener to ask about opener's shape, particularly major-suit length.

---

## When It Applies

**Auction:** 1m – 1M – 1NT – 2♣ or 2<span style="color:red;">♦</span> (the *other* minor = new minor)
**Auction:** 1m – 1M – 2NT – 3♣ or 3<span style="color:red;">♦</span> (the *other* minor = new minor)

- Responder bids the **unbid minor** as an artificial, forcing bid
- Does **not** show clubs/diamonds — it's asking opener to describe further

**Requirements for responder:**
- Invitational or better values (typically 10+ HCP)
- Wants more information before placing the contract

---

## Opener's Responses

Priorities (in order from most important to least):
1. Bid the other major if you have 4+ cards in it.
2. Bid partner's major if you have 3 card support in it.
3. Show your range or shape otherwise


| Opener rebid | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| 2M (responder's major) | 3-card support |
| 2<span style="color:red;">♥</span> (if spades was the major) | 4 hearts |
| 2NT | Minimum, no 3-card major support |
| 3M | 3-card support, maximum |
| 3NT | Maximum, balanced |

## What Is NOT New Minor Forcing

- If partner has not rebid 1NT or 2NT, it's not new minor forcing if you bid a new minor.  Partner will expect that you probably have that minor
- If no majors have been bid before the 1NT or 2NT, it's not new minor forcing
